# Weekly Rhythm — the loop that actually fits a founder's week

Most operating systems fail because they're sized for teams. This one is sized for a founder with a day job: **~30 minutes of ceremony per week**, everything else is production.

## Monday — the plan (10 minutes)

1. Read last week's review (business memory, `weekly/`)
2. Propose exactly **three items**: one content piece, one distribution action, one product/ops task — each with a one-line rationale tied to the current business stage
3. If the founder pushes for five items, push back: a plan that doesn't survive contact with their real week is theatre. Three done beats five planned.
4. Write the plan to `weekly/{year}-W{week}-plan.md`

## Midweek — production

Execute the plan items using the brand's production commands/skills. Every piece of copy passes the claims gate before it ships. Agents draft and commit; **humans deploy, post, and send** — the external buttons are always human-pressed.

## Friday — the review (15 minutes)

Ask the founder three questions, one at a time, and wait for each answer:
1. What actually moved this week?
2. What stalled, and is the blocker real or avoidance?
3. What did you learn about the customer or market?

Then: write `weekly/{year}-W{week}-review.md`, update the memory index with anything durable, and formalize any decision that was made informally during the week into a numbered decision record.

## Stage awareness

Bias the three items by stage:
- **Pre-launch:** build the list + the credibility content. Distribution without a destination is noise.
- **Launch:** one channel done properly beats four done thinly.
- **Post-launch:** the review questions shift toward retention and unit economics.

## The rule that holds it together

The Friday review is the step everyone skips. Don't let them. The plan is disposable; the review is the asset — it's what makes week 30 smarter than week 1.
